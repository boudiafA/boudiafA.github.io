01 / About

The Research Story

I started at Khalifa University working on autonomous robots — teaching machines to navigate through fog, dust, and rocky terrain using only camera input. That early work gave me a deep appreciation for how hard it is to make vision systems robust in the real world.

My PhD pivoted toward foundation models. As transformers and LLMs reshaped the field, I became focused on one question: can we make these powerful models truly useful for domains where data is scarce, labels are expensive, and mistakes have real consequences?

That question led me to agriculture — one of the most data-constrained, high-stakes domains imaginable. I built CropVLM, a VLM that recognises 37 crop species without retraining, and AgriChat, a multimodal chatbot that diagnoses plant disease from a single field image. Both are open-source.

I am recognised on the UAE Research Map and received the of Khalifa University in 2024.

CropVLM

A Vision-Language Model adapted to agriculture via Domain-Specific Semantic Alignment. Trained on 52,987 curated image–caption pairs spanning 37 crop species. HOS-Net enables zero-shot crop detection from text descriptions alone — no retraining required.

Multimodal LLM fine-tuned with LoRA on AgriMM — 121k+ images, 607k+ QA pairs, 3,000+ plant classes. V2VK pipeline eliminates hallucinations via verified phytopathological grounding.
