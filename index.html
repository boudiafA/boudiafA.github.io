01About

Research summary

Designing foundation-model pipelines that translate state-of-the-art AI into scalable, trustworthy tools for sustainable food production.

I'm a PhD candidate in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at Khalifa University of Science and Technology (Abu Dhabi, UAE), working in the Visual Recognition Group under Dr. Sajid Javed.

I started out building autonomous robots — teaching machines to navigate through fog, dust, and rocky terrain using only camera input. That early work gave me a deep appreciation for how hard it is to make vision systems robust in the real world.

My PhD then pivoted toward foundation models. As transformers and LLMs reshaped the field, I focused on one question: can we make these powerful models genuinely useful in domains where data is scarce, labels are expensive, and mistakes carry real consequences?

That question led me to agriculture — one of the most data-constrained, high-stakes domains imaginable. I built CropVLM, a vision–language model that recognises 37 crop species without retraining, and AgriChat, a multimodal assistant that diagnoses plant disease from a single field image. Both are open-source.

CropVLM

A vision–language model adapted to agriculture via Domain-Specific Semantic Alignment, trained on 52,987 curated image–caption pairs across 37 crop species. HOS-Net enables zero-shot crop detection from text descriptions alone — no retraining required.

AgriChat

Multimodal LLM fine-tuned with LoRA on AgriMM — 121k+ images, 607k+ QA pairs, 3,000+ plant classes. The V2VK pipeline eliminates biological hallucinations via verified phytopathological grounding.
